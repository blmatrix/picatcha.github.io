'Caribbean' baroness faces more allegations of cronyism
Published on July 5, 2016 Email To Friend    Print Version

“Partners in crime” (L-R) Lord Patel of Bradford and Baroness Patricia Scotland of Asthal

By Caribbean News Now contributor

LONDON, England -- The new secretary general of the Commonwealth, Dominican-born Baroness Patricia Scotland, is facing yet more allegations of cronyism after awarding a lucrative no-bid contract to a fellow Labour peer within days of taking office.

In an article by Ian Gallagher and Ned Donovan published over the weekend, Britain’s Daily Mail newspaper reported that 'Baroness Brazen' is under fire for handing £90,000 (US$120,000) to KYA Global Ltd, a company owned by her close friend Lord Patel of Bradford, to review her staff's efficiency – and £325,000 (US$432,000) more to to “oversee” its own recommendations carry out the changes.




In a deal criticised by British MPs, Scotland reportedly bypassed the Commonwealth’s normal tendering process by hiring Patel, who Baroness apparently calls her “partner in crime”, as a consultant without putting the job out to other bidders.

Although she cited his company’s “proven track record”, it faced being struck off by the Companies Registrar three years ago because it appeared to be inactive, the Daily Mail reported.

It is unclear how many full-time staff, if any, KYA employs. It does not advertise and has no website or telephone number, and its address is Lord Patel’s home near Bradford. The peer and his wife Yasmin are the firm’s only directors, the newspaper said.

Conservative MP Stuart Andrew said: “My feeling is that if you are going to award a Commonwealth contract, it should not go to someone who is a good friend.”

And Sir Henry Bellingham, chairman of the All-Party Parliamentary Group for the Commonwealth, said it was “essential that proper tendering procedures are followed”.

He added: “There is a conflict of interest in this instance and the contract should be cancelled and put out to tender. Parliament would expect nothing less.”

Scotland took office on April 1 and Patel began his review of the “operations and services” of the Commonwealth Secretariat – responsible for facilitating co-operation between the 53 member states – three days later.

Commonwealth guidelines stipulate that external work should normally be advertised on the Secretariat’s website. Bids are then “evaluated and scored” to determine the most “economically advantageous” tender.

According to the Daily Mail, since Scotland took office, the Secretariat’s procurement manager has resigned because of his dismay at being asked to carry out certain tasks. He declined to comment.

Exactly one month ago, on June 5, 2016, Caribbean News Now asked the procurement section of the Commonwealth Secretariat to confirm or deny the existence of the reported contract with Patel and, if true, to provide us with the dates, amounts and other relevant details.

Despite an acknowledgement of our email, promising a response “shortly”, we are still waiting for a substantive reply.

Another inquiry sent by us on the same date to the Secretariat concerned the appointment of former Barbadian diplomat Lolita Applewhaite as head of Scotland’s private office at the Secretariat.

As previously reported, Applewhaite was heavily involved in Scotland’s campaign for the position of secretary general, including accompanying Scotland on visits to other Caribbean countries.

However, we understand that Applewhaite has exceeded the age at which she should be employed at the Secretariat since, according to our information, other than the secretary general, everyone must be under 60 years of age.

Again, one month later, notwithstanding its professed commitment to transparency, the Secretariat has failed to respond substantively.

Appearances of cronyism on Scotland’s part have since expanded with a report that her newly appointed “head of communications”, Matthew Doyle, handled the propaganda for her Commonwealth campaign.
Meanwhile, Private Eye, a well-known British satirical and current affairs magazine based in London, has weighed in again on the question of the financing for Scotland’s campaign last year for the post of Commonwealth secretary-general.

In its June 10, 2016, issue, Private Eye noted that her campaign was financed by Ron Wahid, a Zurich based financier with a taste for private jets and neo-con politics.

Besides bankrolling her Commonwealth bid through his finance company RJI Capital, he also hired Scotland last year as a “senior adviser” (salary undisclosed) to his secretive consultancy Arcanum Global, which offers “a host of bespoke and tailored strategic intelligence products to government entities and the private sector across a range of industries”.

She joined a team that included Meir Dagan, the former head of the Israeli intelligence service Mossad, before he joined Arcanum as a part-time consultant in January 2011.

A senior US intelligence source remarked to Caribbean News Now separately, “No one is a former anything of Mossad unless they are dead, and even then there is always some doubt.”

Dagan died of cancer earlier this year at the age of 71.

Although Wahid was born in Bangladesh, since moving to America as a boy – and later to Switzerland – he has had no discernible Commonwealth connections, Private Eye noted.

“Whence his sudden interest in being the secretary-general’s Mr Moneybags? This is puzzling British intelligence, among others,” the magazine said.
