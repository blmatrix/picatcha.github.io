14 Facts To Know Before The Mayweather-Pacquiao Fight

The fight of the decade will likely be the highest grossing ever.

February 21st, 2015 Josh Helmuth
Tweet

The fight boxing fans have been wanting for years is now officially just months away. Floyd Mayweather vs. Pacquiao. May 2. MGM Grand in Las Vegas — it’s on. 

Just in case you would like some background going into the spring bout, or just a recap of these legendary fighters and their recent history, here is what you need to know.

Mayweather will receive a 60-40 money split.

This fight is expected to be the highest grossing in history, at $400 million.

Mayweather’s estimated income will be $120 million for the fight, with Pacquiao taking home about $80 million.

Mayweather vs. Pacquiao Statistics

via ESPN

Mayweather (47-0, 26 KOs) has won world titles in 5 weight classes.

Pacquiao (57-5-2, 38 KOs) is the only boxer in history to win world titles in 8 weight divisions.

Pacquiao is an elected congressman in his native of Philippines. 

Pacquiao’s last knockout was against Cotto in 2009.

Mayweather has only knocked out one fighter over the last eight years (Victor Ortiz in 2011)

These two fighters had a fight deal fall apart in the past, with Pacquiao suing Mayweather for defamation for accusing him of using PEDs. The case was eventually settled in court.

Pacquiao and Mayweather are the first two fighters in history to hold crowns in 4 divisions.

If Pacquaio wins, he will become the first man in history to hold crowns in 5 weight classes.

The two fighters met Jan. 27 at a Heat-Bucks game, meeting at halftime to exchange numbers, eventually discussing the fight deal.

As of Friday morning, Mayweather is the Las Vegas favorite, sitting as high as minus-275 at Westgate SuperBook.
