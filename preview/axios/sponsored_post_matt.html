Obama to Trump — stop whining about “rigged” elections

EMILY CLIMER / AP

President Obama says Donald Trump's allegations of a "rigged" election is "unprecedented," and that Trump's attitude "doesn't really show the leadership and toughness" that he'd need to be a successful president. "[If] you start whining before the election is even over … you don't have what it takes to be in this job," Obama said at a press conference.

  • Get Serious: Obama also said there is "no serious person out there who would suggest that you could even rig America's election," since they're run by state and local officials, many of them Republicans."
  • How Democracy Survives": If Hillary Clinton loses, Obama said, she'll give a "gracious concessions speech" and Obama will welcome Trump to the White House. "That's how democracy survives, because we recognize that there's something more important than any individual's campaign." Obama said.
